<p class="lead">There&rsquo;s one app I keep building. Mood, habits, tasks, a journal, all in one place, for brains that can&rsquo;t hold all four at once. I&rsquo;ve built it about fifteen times.</p>

Not fixed fifteen times. Built. New repo, new stack, new name, every time. First one was meh-trics, &ldquo;for chaotic goblin lifestyles.&rdquo; Then it became the Executive Dysfunction Center, which I rebuilt on a new stack every time because every month a new tool showed up promising to make it easier. Encore, Supabase, v0, Leap, I tried all of them. Same app, different scaffolding, all archived by November.

When the all-in-one stalled I chopped it into pieces and built each piece as its own app. A calendar. A task list. A journal. Most of those got archived too.

In February I got tired of the weight and built nibble, &ldquo;three files, no build step, no node_modules.&rdquo; A planner I actually use. In April I changed the question: instead of logging your mood in a form, the new ones let you talk and an AI ghostwrites the journal at the end of the day. One&rsquo;s called meat-suit and bills itself as a decompression chamber. That&rsquo;s the live version.

The dead ones are all still there, archived, in date order. Cheaper than therapy and worse documented.
